About Me

I am a Machine Learning Researcher pursuing my Ph.D. in Computational Modeling and Simulation at the University of Pittsburgh. My research centers on scientific machine learning (SciML), focusing on modeling complex systems and solving challenging partial differential equations (PDEs). I leverage techniques such as scalable Reduced-Order Modeling, achieving 1000x acceleration for nonlinear stochastic PDEs, and Deep Learning-based approaches, leading to 100x faster predictions for dynamical systems. I am proficient in Python, JAX, TensorFlow, and various ML/DL paradigms, including generative models and LLM fine-tuning. My project experience includes developing foundation models, applying Gaussian Processes, and utilizing Vision Transformers for dynamical systems prediction. My published research has accumulated over 350 citations, and I hold multiple awards, including a CMS Fellowship and the Best National Master's Thesis Award in Aerospace Engineering.
