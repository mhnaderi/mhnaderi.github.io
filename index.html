About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Computational Modeling and Simulation at the University of Pittsburgh, passionate about leveraging machine learning to understand and control complex physical phenomena. My work sits at the intersection of deep learning, scientific computing, and engineering, focusing on developing robust and scalable data-driven models. With a strong foundation in aerospace engineering and aerodynamics, I bring a unique perspective to applying advanced algorithms like Reinforcement Learning, Evolutional Deep Neural Networks, and Reduced-Order Modeling to challenging problems in fluid dynamics, chaotic systems, and PDE solutions. I am proficient in Python, JAX, and TensorFlow and dedicated to producing high-impact research, evidenced by publications in top-tier journals and over 350 citations.
